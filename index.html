▚ BOOTH LOG · 0731-Q3

> WHY THESE TOPICS LANDED

Every topic here was built the same way, on purpose. We start from the questions our buyers are actually asking and the categories we most need to own: Managed Solutions, outcome-based pricing, global hiring, and AI inside the workflow. Then we match each topic to the author with the most credibility to answer it. Audience first, lane second. The ones marked Recommended are the easiest place to start: clear, definitional pieces you could explain in your sleep that also happen to be what AI engines pick up first. We'll keep sharpening the picks with live HubSpot data as we go.

📡Where we're starting

The AEO score (0–100, higher is better) is how visible and well-represented Booth is in each engine's answers. SoV = share of voice, how often Booth shows up versus competitors.

On the +33% goal: this is a 33% relative increase in share of voice over the July baseline, not a rise of 33 percentage points. We re-measure monthly with the AEO Grader and report the movement into the BoothGrowth Rock.

The gap

It isn't perception — AI engines already describe Booth favorably, and on people-first questions we're the name they give. It's presence. That's why we lead with definitional pieces that teach the engines who Booth is, and re-check the score monthly against the +33% goal.

👀 How we show up today

On the questions that fit Booth, ChatGPT already ranks Booth #1 for both people-first and mid-market managed outsourcing, and Perplexity names only Booth for a people-first partner. On generic and enterprise-BPO queries we're still absent — and the definitional questions (what is an EOR, managed vs. staff augmentation) name no vendor yet, which is exactly our opening.
